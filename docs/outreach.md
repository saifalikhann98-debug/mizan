# Outreach — collecting the first real prices

Goal: get ~15–20 real, buy-side prices into ONE bucket (AC service, Al Qusais corridor) to
test whether people will actually share what they paid. Read like a person, not a survey.
End every message in a way that makes replying a number frictionless.

---

## 1. WhatsApp — to your own network (best converter, send first)
Short, casual, asks for one specific thing.

> Quick one — getting my AC cleaned and the quotes are all over the place 😅
> What did you actually pay last time, and which area? Trying to figure out what's fair
> before someone overcharges me. Just the number + area is perfect 🙏

Variant (if they've helped before / closer friends):

> Doing a little thing to help people not get ripped off on AC cleaning in Dubai.
> If you remember — what did you pay last time and where? One number, that's all I need.

---

## 2. WhatsApp — broadcast / status
For a status post or a broadcast list. Lower pressure, casual.

> AC cleaning season 🥵 — collecting what people *actually* pay (not the inflated quotes).
> Reply with what you paid + your area and I'll send back whether you got a fair deal.

---

## 3. Facebook expat group post (harvest + recruit at once)
Post in Dubai expat / area groups. The trick: give value first (you'll share the range),
then the number is the price of entry. Reply to each commenter with where they landed.

> **What's a fair price for AC cleaning in Dubai right now?**
> Quotes I'm getting range from AED 150 to 600+ for the same job, which is mad.
> Trying to build an honest picture: if you've had yours cleaned recently, drop
> **what you paid + your area** below. I'll reply to everyone with whether that's
> fair vs what others are paying. Let's stop people getting overcharged 🙏

---

## 4. Replying inside "how much should X cost?" threads (ongoing harvest)
When someone else asks the question, you answer with value AND pull a price:

> From what people are reporting, a fair range in [area] is around AED [X–Y] for a standard
> split unit. If you don't mind — what did you end up paying? Adding it helps the next person
> get a straight answer too.

---

## Rules for all of the above
- One ask, one number. Don't ask for a survey.
- Always tie it to a moment of need (getting a quote, just paid).
- Buy-side only — what they *paid*, not what a company quoted as a list price.
- Log every reply into the **one bucket** (AC / Al Qusais corridor) until it passes 5 real prices.
- Track honestly: did prices come easily, or did you have to beg? That's the real test.
